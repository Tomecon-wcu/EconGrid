Model Wars

This site showcases a competition between interactive market simulations built by undergraduate students in Principles of Microeconomics using Anthropic's Claude.ai — designed to visualize how markets respond to supply and demand shocks.

About This Project

Students enrolled in Principles of Microeconomics (ECO112) worked with Anthropic's Claude.ai to design and build interactive, supply and demand models. Each student specified a real-world market and constructed a simulation grounded in plausible economic values.

Each model begins at a clearly defined equilibrium — before the participant triggers a market disruption using a slider control. The goal is to tell a coherent economic story: from initial equilibrium, through a market shift, to the adjustment process that restores equilibrium.

🤖 AI-Assisted Construction

Models were built through iterative dialogue with Claude.ai. Students specified market conditions and refined the output to produce the desired economic analysis.

📊 Interactive by Design

Each model features a slider that triggers a supply or demand shift, revealing the resulting shortage or surplus and the path to a new equilibrium.

🎯 Grounded in Reality

Students anchored their models to real markets with realistic price and quantity levels, adding realism to economic logic.

🏆 Peer Evaluation

Completed models are evaluated through a head-to-head tournament — Model Wars — in which participants judge pairs of models on accuracy, clarity, and instructional quality.

Model Requirements

To ensure consistency and pedagogical rigor, each model should demonstrate all of the following features. These same criteria guide peer evaluation in Model Wars.

  • 1
    A well-specified market — a named good with realistic equilibrium price and quantity values.
  • 2
    An interactive slider — controls the magnitude of a supply or demand shift in real time.
  • 3
    Shortage or surplus visualization — the gap between Qs and Qd at the original price is clearly shown.
  • 4
    Market adjustment arrows — directional cues showing how price, Qd, and Qs move toward the new equilibrium.
  • 5
    Labeled equilibria — both the original and new equilibrium points are identified.
  • 6
    Market analysis summary — a text panel reporting the quantitative changes in price and quantity.

Sample Model — Beef Market

The model below is a representative example of the type of simulation students produced. It depicts the U.S. beef market and shows what happens when consumer demand increases — driven, for example, by rising incomes or changing dietary preferences. Use the slider to shift the demand curve rightward, observe the resulting shortage at the original price, and watch the market adjust to a new equilibrium. As price rises, quantity demanded falls while quantity supplied rises.
