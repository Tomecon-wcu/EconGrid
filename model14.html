What this model shows: When supply increases, the supply curve shifts rightward from S₁ to S₂. At the original price P₁, producers now supply more than consumers demand — creating a temporary surplus. Market pressure drives the price down along S₂ (more quantity supplied) and consumers respond by buying more along D (more quantity demanded), until a new equilibrium E₂ is reached at a lower price P₂ and higher quantity Q₂.
