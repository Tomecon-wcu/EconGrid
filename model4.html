How to Read This Model

  1. Drag the slider right to increase market supply — the supply curve shifts from S to S′.
  2. At the original price of $6.00, the rightward shift creates a surplus (quantity supplied > quantity demanded). The gold arrow shows this horizontal surplus.
  3. The surplus puts downward pressure on price. The market adjusts toward the new equilibrium at $4.00.
  4. Along the demand curve (D), the red arrow traces the decrease in quantity demanded as price falls.
  5. Along the new supply curve (S′), the teal arrow traces the increase in quantity supplied as producers respond to the lower price.
  6. The new equilibrium settles at $4.00 and 2.7 billion pounds.
