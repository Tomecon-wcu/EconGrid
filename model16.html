U.S. Goat Meat Market
Effect of an Increase in Demand

Principles of Microeconomics · Demand Shift Analysis

A supply and demand graph for the U.S. goat meat market. The original equilibrium is at $3.50 per pound and 68 million pounds. When demand increases, the demand curve shifts right from D1 to D2. At the old price of $3.50, a shortage appears. Price then rises along the supply curve to the new equilibrium at approximately $4.25 per pound and 72 million pounds. Use the slider to control the size of the demand increase.
